Expiration Sticker Sheet

Found behind the lower hinge, adhesive side protected by a hairnet wrapper. The dates are not mystical; they are inventory language written small enough to survive supervisors.

  • Yellow stickers mark stock that must leave first, not stock that is bad.
  • Orange stickers are for wrapper corners, especially crackers that puff in wet weather.
  • Blue stickers belong to new delivery trays; do not reuse them on old candy unless you want a month of false confidence.
  • Pink stickers were a joke until the yogurt pretzels arrived warm.

If the sticker curls like a wood shaving, rotate stock before debating the date. If it falls off clean, the package is sweating.
