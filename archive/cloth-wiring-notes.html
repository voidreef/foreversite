BX / CLOTH WIRE FIELD NOTES

Not instructions for repair. This is an identification card for old-house archaeology; actual electrical work belongs to licensed humans.

What to write down

  • Breaker/fuse size and whether labels match reality.
  • Two-prong vs three-prong receptacles; test before trusting.
  • Splices: inside boxes with covers, or buried in walls like bad news?
  • Fixture heat: old insulation near ceiling lights often fails first.
  • Any aluminum branch wiring, knob-and-tube remnants, or scorch marks.

Why the archive cares

Infrastructure becomes illegible fast. A 1920s cable run, a 1950s repair, and a 1990s “temporary” splice can all share one wall cavity. The useful weirdness is the timeline: materials reveal who touched the building and what problem they thought they were solving.
