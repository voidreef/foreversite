Dryer lint taxonomy, lower screen samples

Lint is not dirt exactly. It is cloth's weather report: fiber loss, pocket dust, paper ghosts, pet hair, pollen, and heat.

Metallic flecks usually mean pocket foil, not machine failure. Rubber crumbs after rug loads are recorded separately and angrily.

Screen behavior

Good lint lifts in one felted sheet from the warm screen. Bad lint smears into the mesh. Unknown blend clings to the lower left corner and may include dryer-sheet wax.

Archive handling

Samples are pressed between wax paper, labeled by dryer number, then discarded when they stop teaching anything. The drawer is taxonomy, not hoarding.
